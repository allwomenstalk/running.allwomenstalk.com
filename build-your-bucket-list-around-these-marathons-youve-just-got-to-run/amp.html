Build Your Bucket List around These 26 Marathons Youve Just Got to Run ...

By Jennifer • 2 Comments

If you're a marathoner, you know how hellish it can be to spend so many hours trudging along a boring course. Luckily, not all marathons are created equal - some are so stellar, you've just got to add them to your bucket list, stat! Which one will you train for first? Let's find out!

1 Athens Marathon

Register at: athensauthenticmarathon.gr
According to legend, Athens is the site of the very first marathon in 490 AD; the finish line is at Panathinaiko Stadium, built for the first modern Olympics in 1896. If you want a little history with your run, this is the marathon to do.

***

The Athens Marathon is one of the oldest and most historic marathons in the world. It follows the route that the Athenian soldier Pheidippides ran from the town of Marathon to Athens to deliver the news of the Greek victory over the Persians. The race takes place every November and attracts thousands of runners from around the world. The course starts in the town of Marathon and finishes at the Panathinaiko Stadium in Athens, where the first modern Olympic Games were held. This marathon offers a unique opportunity to experience the rich history and culture of Athens while also challenging yourself physically.

2 Bermuda Marathon

Register at: bermudaraceweekend.com
The last thing most people want to do on their island vacation is sweat, but you're a runner, so this course would appeal to you on so many levels!

***

The Bermuda Marathon is a popular race that takes place during the Bermuda Race Weekend. This event is organized by the Bermuda Tourism Authority and attracts runners from all over the world. The course takes runners through the stunning scenery of the island, including pink sand beaches, lush greenery, and historic landmarks. The race also offers a variety of distances to choose from, including a full marathon, half marathon, and 10K. Participants can also enjoy post-race festivities, including live music and local cuisine. The Bermuda Marathon is a must-do for any avid runner looking to combine their love for the sport with a tropical vacation.

3 Big Five Marathon

Register at: big-five-marathon.com
It's part grueling marathon, part wildlife safari, together an amazing, once-in-a-lifetime experience. You'll literally be running through an African reserve. How cool is that?

***

The Big Five Marathon is a unique and challenging marathon that takes place in the African savannah, offering runners the chance to experience a wildlife safari while pushing their physical limits. The race takes place in the Entabeni Game Reserve in South Africa, where runners will encounter the "big five" animals - lions, elephants, buffaloes, leopards, and rhinos - as they make their way through the course. The marathon is known for its tough terrain, with steep hills and rocky paths, making it a true test of endurance. It is also a great opportunity to immerse oneself in the stunning natural beauty of Africa and its diverse wildlife.

4 Big Sur International Marathon

Register at: bsim.org
Renowned for its beauty (and its difficult hills), this marathon has a 6-hour cutoff and intensely gorgeous views.

***

The Big Sur International Marathon, held in California, is a must-do for any avid runner. Known for its challenging hills and stunning scenery, this marathon has a strict 6-hour cutoff time and offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean and the iconic Bixby Bridge. The course takes runners through the charming towns of Carmel and Big Sur, offering a unique and memorable experience. Registration for this popular event can be found on their website, bsim.org. Don't miss out on the opportunity to add this bucket list-worthy marathon to your running goals.

5 Boston Marathon

Register at: baa.org
It's America's most iconic marathon event, and the first one ever run by a woman, Kathy Switzer, in 1967.

***

The Boston Marathon, organized by the Boston Athletic Association, is one of the oldest and most prestigious marathons in the world. It takes place on Patriots' Day, a holiday in Massachusetts, and attracts over 30,000 runners from all over the world. The course is known for its challenging terrain, including the infamous "Heartbreak Hill," and its rich history, with the first race being held in 1897. In addition to being the first marathon to allow women to officially compete, the Boston Marathon is also known for its strict qualifying times and competitive atmosphere. It is a must-run event for serious runners and an unforgettable experience for all who participate.

6 Chicago Marathon

Register at: chicagomarathon.com
This is a big-city marathon without the typical headaches of traffic and boredom. It's in mid-October, when the weather is iffy: you can expect a winter chill with wind/snow, or incredibly hot and humid, so train for both.

***

The Chicago Marathon, held in mid-October, is a popular choice for runners looking for a big-city marathon experience. Unlike other major marathons, the Chicago Marathon boasts a relatively easy registration process and minimal traffic disruptions. However, the weather can be unpredictable, ranging from cold and windy to hot and humid. This makes it important for runners to train for all conditions. The course takes runners through some of the city's most iconic landmarks, including the beautiful Lake Michigan waterfront. With its flat and fast course, the Chicago Marathon is also a popular choice for runners looking to achieve a personal best time.

7 Country Music Marathon

Register at: runrocknroll.com
If you're a fan of country, or you just want to run for a good cause (St. Jude Children's Research Hospital), then this is the marathon for you, held in the heart of country music - Nashville.

***

The Country Music Marathon, also known as the St. Jude Rock 'n' Roll Nashville Marathon, is an annual marathon held in Nashville, Tennessee. It is organized by the Rock 'n' Roll Marathon Series and benefits St. Jude Children's Research Hospital. The course takes runners through the iconic sights of Nashville, including the famous Music Row and the Country Music Hall of Fame. In addition to the full marathon, there is also a half marathon and a 5K race available. The event draws thousands of participants each year and is a must-do for any country music fan or runner looking to support a good cause.

8 Detroit Marathon

Register at: freepmarathon.com
I'm partial to this marathon because I was born in the Motor City, but also because I love the fast, flat course, winding through downtown, across the Ambassador Bridge to Canada, then back to Detroit through the Tunnel. It's so cool!

***

The Detroit Marathon, also known as the Detroit Free Press Marathon, is an annual event that takes place in October. It is one of the largest marathons in the United States, with over 25,000 runners participating each year. The course is known for its fast and flat terrain, making it a popular choice for runners looking to achieve a personal best time. The race starts and finishes in downtown Detroit, and also takes runners across the Ambassador Bridge to Canada and back through the Detroit-Windsor Tunnel. The marathon offers a unique experience with stunning views of the city and international border crossings.

9 Great Wall Marathon

Register at: great-wall-marathon.com
Since 1999, this grueling marathon has challenged runners with more than 5,000 steep and crumbling steps, but vistas that will take your breath away (as much as the course itself).

***

The Great Wall Marathon is a highly sought after marathon that has been taking place since 1999. It is known for its challenging course, which includes over 5,000 steep and crumbling steps, but also offers breathtaking views of the Great Wall of China. The race is open to runners of all levels, with options for a full marathon, half marathon, and 8.5km Fun Run. Participants can also choose to run for charity, with proceeds going towards preserving the Great Wall. The event draws in runners from all over the world and has become a must-do for many marathon enthusiasts.

10 Honolulu Marathon

Register at:honolulumarathon.org
I can't imagine a more beautiful place to run than Hawaii, and this course includes magnificent ocean views alongside Waikiki Beach, and Diamond Head and Koko Head volcanic craters.

***

The Honolulu Marathon is one of the most popular and iconic races in the world, attracting over 30,000 runners each year. It takes place in December, making it the perfect destination for a winter getaway and a great way to end the year with a bang. The course is relatively flat and fast, making it ideal for both experienced and novice runners. The race also has a strong community feel, with local volunteers and spectators cheering on participants throughout the course. And, as if running in paradise wasn't enough, the finish line is located just steps away from the famous Waikiki Beach, where runners can relax and recover after their 26.2-mile journey.

11 Houston Marathon

Register at: chevronhoustonmarathon.com
Looking for a hard-to-find January event, one where you might just score your own PB? This fast and flat course may be exactly what you want!

***

.

The Houston Marathon, held annually in January, is a highly sought-after event for runners looking to achieve a personal best time. The course is known for being fast and flat, making it ideal for setting new records. Registration for the marathon can be done through their website, and it's recommended to sign up early as spots tend to fill up quickly. The event is organized by the Houston Marathon Committee, a non-profit organization that also raises funds for various charities. The marathon attracts both elite runners and amateurs alike, creating a diverse and competitive atmosphere. Don't miss out on the chance to run this iconic race and potentially achieve your own PB!

12 London Marathon

Register at: virginmoneylondonmarathon.com
This course is more known for its upbeat party atmosphere than its toughness, so why not get in a little sight-seeing with your run?

***

The London Marathon is one of the most iconic races in the world, attracting over 40,000 runners each year. It takes place in April and starts at Greenwich Park, winding its way through the city and passing by famous landmarks such as the Tower of London, Buckingham Palace, and the London Eye. The course is relatively flat, making it a great option for first-time marathoners or those looking to set a personal record. The race also has a lively and festive atmosphere, with spectators lining the streets and cheering on the runners. Additionally, the London Marathon is a major fundraising event for various charities, making it a meaningful and rewarding experience for participants.

13 Loon Mountain Race

Register at: acidoticracing.com
This course is ridiculously challenging - you'll literally run up a mountain- but if up you're tired of fast, flat courses, give ti a go!

***

The Loon Mountain Race, located in New Hampshire, is a must-do for any avid runner. This challenging course takes participants up the mountain, providing a unique and thrilling experience. Registration for the race can be found on the Acidotic Racing website. While the course may be difficult, it offers a refreshing change from the typical flat courses. In addition, the race is known for its stunning views of the White Mountains. Don't miss out on this opportunity to push yourself and take part in one of the most iconic races in the country.

14 Marabana Havana Marathon

Register at: havanamarathon.net
You'll need a special amateur sports license (issued by the U.S. Department of Treasury) to run this race in Cuba, but it's worth the extra hassle to run a course very few Americans have seen.

***

The Marabana Havana Marathon is a unique and exciting race that should definitely be on every runner's bucket list. Held in the vibrant city of Havana, Cuba, this marathon offers a chance to experience the country's rich culture and history while pushing yourself to the limit. However, American runners should note that they will need to obtain a special amateur sports license from the U.S. Department of Treasury in order to participate. This added step may seem like a hassle, but it's worth it to be one of the few Americans to run this course and make unforgettable memories in a country that is still relatively unexplored by U.S. travelers. Don't miss out on the opportunity to be a part of this unique and challenging marathon!

15 Marathon Du Medoc

Register at: marathondumedoc.com
Wine-loving marathoners, rejoice! This unique course winds through gorgeous vineyards and includes aid stations serving wine, oysters, foie gras, cheese and ice cream.

***

The Marathon Du Medoc is a must-do for wine enthusiasts and marathon runners alike. This race takes place in the picturesque vineyards of Bordeaux, France and offers a unique experience for participants. In addition to the typical aid stations with water and energy gels, runners can also indulge in wine, oysters, foie gras, cheese, and even ice cream along the course. The event also features a costume contest, making it a fun and festive atmosphere. Registration for the race can be found on the official website, marathondumedoc.com. Don't miss out on this one-of-a-kind marathon experience!

16 Midnight Sun Marathon

Register at: msm.no
Wouldn't it be great to run a marathon at night, in bright sunshine, in beautiful Norway?

***

The Midnight Sun Marathon, held annually in Tromsø, Norway, is a unique and unforgettable experience for runners. The race takes place in the middle of the night, but with the sun still shining bright. This phenomenon is due to the location of Tromsø, which is located above the Arctic Circle and experiences the midnight sun during the summer months. The marathon course offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and fjords, making it a scenic and challenging race. In addition to the full marathon, there are also options for a half marathon, 10K, and kids' race. The event also includes a festival atmosphere with live music and food vendors. Don't miss your chance to add this one-of-a-kind marathon to your bucket list.

17 Moab Trail Marathon

Register at: moabtrailmarathon.com
Utah is one of the most beautiful places in the world, and this race runs right through Moab's canyons and red stone arches.

***

The Moab Trail Marathon is a must-run race for any avid runner, especially those looking for a scenic and challenging course. Located in Utah, this marathon takes you through the stunning canyons and iconic red stone arches of Moab. With a variety of distances to choose from, including a full marathon, half marathon, and 5k, there is something for every level of runner. The race also offers stunning views of the Colorado River and the La Sal Mountains. Registration can be found on the official website, moabtrailmarathon.com. Don't miss out on this unforgettable race experience.

18 New York City Marathon

Register at: tcsnycmarathon.org
This is the world's largest marathon, running through all five of the city's boroughs and finishing in Central Park.

***

The New York City Marathon is a must-run event for any avid runner. It is the largest marathon in the world and attracts over 50,000 participants each year. The course takes runners through all five boroughs of the city, showcasing its diverse neighborhoods and iconic landmarks such as the Brooklyn Bridge and Times Square. The marathon is also known for its electric atmosphere, with enthusiastic crowds lining the streets to cheer on the runners. The race finishes in Central Park, providing a scenic and memorable end to the 26.2-mile journey. Registration for the marathon can be done through the official website, tcsnycmarathon.org.

19 Patagonia International Marathon

Register at: patagonianinternationalmarathon.com
This marathon is held to raise awareness of conservation efforts of the Patagonian region, so for every participant, a tree is planted.

***

The Patagonia International Marathon is a must-run event for any avid marathoner. Held in the stunning Patagonian region, this marathon not only offers breathtaking views but also supports a great cause. For every participant, a tree is planted to help with conservation efforts in the area. The marathon route takes runners through Torres del Paine National Park, a UNESCO World Biosphere Reserve, showcasing the beauty of this untouched landscape. The race offers three different distances to choose from - 10K, half marathon, and full marathon - making it accessible to runners of all levels. Don't miss out on the opportunity to be a part of this unique and impactful event.

20 Pike's Peak Marathon

Register at: pikespeakmarathon.org
It's hard to imagine a course more difficult than this, especially if you're not used to trail running and running in high altitudes. Though the race is in August, there's the possibility you'll be running in heat at first, then as you ascend, several inches of snow.

***

The Pike's Peak Marathon is a must-do for any avid runner looking for a challenge. This marathon takes place in August and covers a grueling 26.2-mile course through the Rocky Mountains in Colorado. The race starts at an elevation of 6,300 feet and finishes at the summit of Pike's Peak, which stands at 14,115 feet. The steep inclines and high altitude make this marathon one of the toughest in the world. Participants must be prepared for unpredictable weather conditions, including heat, snow, and strong winds. Registration for the Pike's Peak Marathon is open on the official website, pikespeakmarathon.org.

21 Portland Marathon

Register at: portlandmarathon.org
If you prefer cooler temps, and an environmentally-friendly, fun atmosphere, combined with a relatively flat course, this is the race for you - swag includes the typical medal and tee, but also a seedling.

***

The Portland Marathon, held annually in October, is a must-run race for those who enjoy cooler temperatures and a sustainable race environment. The course is relatively flat, making it ideal for runners of all levels. In addition to the typical race swag of a medal and t-shirt, participants also receive a seedling to plant. This race is known for its fun atmosphere and has been recognized for its commitment to being environmentally-friendly. Registration can be found on their website, portlandmarathon.org. Don't miss out on this unique and exciting marathon experience!

22 Reggae Marathon

Register at: reggaemarathon.com
It's tradition: at the end of the race, slip off your shoes and run right into the ocean. During the race, reggae music plays at every mile... sounds like fun!

***

The Reggae Marathon is an annual event held in Negril, Jamaica and is known as one of the most unique and fun marathons in the world. The race takes place on the first Saturday of December and offers a full marathon, half marathon, and 10k option for runners of all levels. The course takes runners along the stunning coastline of Negril, with reggae music playing at every mile to keep participants energized and entertained. After crossing the finish line, runners have the opportunity to continue the tradition of running straight into the ocean to cool off and celebrate their accomplishment. The event also features a pasta party the night before the race and a beach party afterwards, making it a truly unforgettable experience.

23 Reykjavik Marathon

Register at: marathon.is
Nearly everyone who lives in Iceland turns out as both runners and spectators at this race, which runs through the city and past gorgeous waterfront and natural geothermal springs.

***

The Reykjavik Marathon is an annual event that takes place in the capital city of Iceland. It is a popular race that attracts both runners and spectators from all over the country. The course takes runners through the city, providing them with stunning views of the waterfront and natural geothermal springs. The marathon is organized by Reykjavik Sports Union and has been running since 1984. It offers a variety of distances, including a full marathon, half marathon, and shorter fun runs for families and children. The race is known for its friendly and inclusive atmosphere, making it a must-do for any avid runner's bucket list.

24 Rock 'n' Roll Las Vegas Marathon

Register at: runrocknroll.com
All the energy and fun of the Vegas strip at night - it's like a party with your PB!

***

The Rock 'n' Roll Las Vegas Marathon is a must-run race for any marathon enthusiast. This iconic event takes place at night, allowing runners to experience the vibrant energy and excitement of the Vegas strip. With live music and entertainment along the course, this marathon is like a party while also challenging runners to achieve their personal best. The race offers a variety of distances, including a full marathon, half marathon, and 10k, making it accessible for runners of all levels. Plus, the city of Las Vegas offers endless opportunities for post-race celebrations and relaxation. Register now at runrocknroll.com to secure your spot in this unforgettable marathon experience.

25 Toronto Waterfront Marathon

Register at: torontowaterfrontmarathon.com
Run along the shores of Lake Ontario in this race, marked out in kilometers, not miles.

***

The Toronto Waterfront Marathon is an annual event that takes place in Toronto, Canada. It is a popular race among runners, known for its scenic route along the beautiful shores of Lake Ontario. The race is marked in kilometers, not miles, making it a unique experience for participants. It is open to runners of all levels, from beginners to elite athletes. The event also offers a half marathon and 5k race for those who prefer shorter distances. In addition to the race, the event also includes a Health and Fitness Expo, where participants can browse and purchase running gear and accessories. The marathon is known for its energetic and supportive atmosphere, making it a must-do for any runner's bucket list.
